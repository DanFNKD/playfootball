Why play football?

There are many reasons to get involved. We've broken down some of the key physical and mental benefits below:

Improve your fitness

Football involves continuous running and sprinting which in turn improves your cardiovascular health and fitness.

Lose weight

During a typical 90 minute football match, a player will burn an estimated 700 calories. This will help you to burn excess body fat and maintain a healthy body weight.

Increased balance and coordination

Improving core technical skills such as dribbling and passing will increase your balance and coordination.

Better bone health

Football is a high impact sport. Activities such as running, jumping and tackling strengthens bones.

Stress reduction

Playing sport triggers the release of endorphins, chemicals in the brain that naturally lift your mood, reduce stress and promote relaxation.

Boosted self confidence

Honing your skills, improving performance and achieving success will buil your self-confidence and self-esteem.

Social connection

Football provides the opportunity for social interaction, camaraderie and relationship building. Being part of a team fosters a sense of belonging and community.

Resilience and perseverance

On your journey, you'll face challenges, setbacks and failures. Learning to bounce back from these builds resilience and mental fortitude.
